What is init(); ?

Init is a platform for students to showcase their innovative business Ideas / Prototype to other people, In this way they can get their first customers and also get to know how people are going to digest their ideas in real life.

What We Do

We organize this event, which goes by the name 'init();', where we select few promising founders with mind-blowing ideas and give them a platform to showcase their stuff and atrract their 'first' customers.

Why is the name ' init(); '?

init is the short for "initialization". We help founders make their dream project at the early-stage, helping them to initialize the whole process, by providing them with tools, technical help, and tips to kick-start their startup.

Roadmap

Here's how we select teams:

Stage 1 : The teams will register online with team details for an introductory session on the challenge. This registration automatically registers them for the main event. The introductory session will brief the participants about this new kind of competition. We will also be showing some sample business plans for their easy reference. All of their doubts will cleared in this round. Participants will be introduce to the criteria of judgement and will be given a few advices about the challenge. This round might also be done online with the help of Google+ Hangouts.

Stage 2 : After the round 1 when participants have an essence about the competition. They will be given 3 weeks to prepare. During this period they will have to do a thorough research about their business and make the business plan.

In this round each team will explain their innovative business to the investors and give an impressive presentation. Giving a presentation doesn't does not mean preparing a power point show. Participants can use any kind of presentation tool such as prezi, a website, video etc. though showing a logo of the start-up company or showing the number of likes or followers on social networking sites etc. adds up a star to their work. Each team will be given 8 minutes for the presentation. Teams will be judged upon their business popularity, clarity about their view of business, confidence, figures etc. Only the teams which submit the business plan will be allowed to enter this round.

Stage 3 : On the day of round 2, teams will be required to bring their business plan along with them and submit it to the judges. Their business plans will then be judged overnight. The business plan should be the brain of their business. It will contain each and everything about their business. This is the major part of the competition.

Stage 4 : Only the top 6 teams get selected in this round, the others get eliminated but will still be called as audience. This round will just like be a job interview session. Teams will have to be completely prepared for a fire-ball of questions. Cross questioning by the judges will take place in this round about their business. Each team member should be completely prepared for any kind of question about their business. Judges can also refer to their business plan for this round.
